Overview

Depending on the project, client, time constraints, team dynamics and budget I strive to follow the below design thinking philosophy. Under optimal conditions each phase should include sufficient ideation within each phase and between each phase.

Empathize

The initial phase is crucial for determining high-level needs. I start by asking questions, which may sound like: what are the pain points and what are the frustrations. By listening to stakeholders and users I begin to formulate an increasingly accurate problem statement. This is an on-going process that is revisited when new problems are discovered, which may require the team to adapt a different approach. This phase can include (but not limited to):

  • Surveys
  • Stakeholder and customer interviews
  • Contextual Inquiries
  • Direct Observation

Behavior Used: Framing

Define

With the information gathered from the Empathize phase, I begin to identify patterns and organize data into different categories and priorities. This is an on-going process that is revisited when new information is discovered, which may require the team to adapt a different approach. This phase can include (but not limited to):

  • Functional Specification Documentation (FSD)
  • Persona Creatiion
  • Sitemaps
  • Style Guides

Behavior Used: Framing

Ideate

From the information gathered from the Empathize and Define phases I begin to sketch-out concepts of possible solutions. This is an on-going process that is revisited when new information is discovered. Procedures can include (but not limited to):

  • Experience Map
  • Task Flows
  • Wireframes

Behavior Used: Risk

Prototype

I believe prototyping is about asking the right questions, not building models of answers. Within the right culture prototyping should be more about problem finding than problem solving. Building prototypes that fail is part of innovation. This should not be misunderstand as bringing failures to market. The key is to fail early, fail quick and fail cheap. Eventually prototyping is a way to converge on the most optimal design. This is an on-going process that is revisited when new information is discovered. This phase can include (but not limited to):

  • Dark Horse Prototyping
  • Interactive Wireframing
  • Interactive PDF
  • Paper Prototyping

Behavior Used: Choice

Test

Upon completion & implementation of the final design, testing is necessary to evaluate the effectiveness of the design purpose. This may include acceptance testing, usability testing and questionnaires

Behavior Used: Choice
