Marc is an independent UX Designer, Information Architect and Usability Analyst with a focus on cognitive and pre-attentive user experience solutions that improve conversion rates, customer engagement and brand loyalty.

With over 15 years experience serving the Internet and another five helping to make businesses become more profitable and streamlined, Marc possesses the unique combination of user experience expertise, business acumen, and design thinking that allow him to create innovative solutions for companies, clients and users.

Marc is author of the popular blog UX Acrobat, where he attempts to move the UX community to realize the difference between academic UX and business UX by sharing his insights about things few UX practitioners seem to want to say.

Comfortable working across design, development and usability teams, Marc develops anything from wireframes, task flows, clickable and paper prototypes, personas, heuristic evaluations and usability testing.

Marc believes the intersection of game mechanics, cognitive science and design thinking build success and should be a core business strategy for brands looking to maximize ROI, increase customer satisfaction and build lasting relationship.

Before his UX life, Marc was a Business Analyst in Asia, in the consumer goods industry where he worked on everything from quality control to product development to lab product testing, to process and system modeling and improvement.

Marc earned a BA (with distinction) in Int'l Relations and a Master's degree in Technological System Management from the School of Engineering at New York State University at Stony Brook.
